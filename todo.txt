Draft for this section
- Primary peril for MVP: pluvial and fluvial flood risk monitoring.
- Pilot geography: Romania, Timis County (Timisoara metro and nearby flood-prone zones).
- Primary user profile: insurer catastrophe underwriter who needs zone and asset-level risk inputs for pricing and portfolio steering.
- Secondary user profile: claims operations analyst who needs near-real-time impact triage and prioritization after severe rainfall events.
- Success metric (MVP): at least 80% overlap between high-risk flagged zones and known affected zones in one historical flood case, with 24 to 48 hours lead-time alerting before event peak.
- Problem statement (draft): Insurers operating in climate-exposed regions face growing flood losses while still relying on delayed, fragmented, and low-granularity signals for underwriting and claims decisions. WaterCore - weilding Copernicus addresses this by combining Copernicus Earth Observation and Galileo or EGNOS-aligned geospatial evidence to produce explainable, near-real-time flood risk intelligence at zone level, enabling more accurate pricing, faster event response, and improved portfolio resilience.
