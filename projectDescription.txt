Project Title
WaterCore - weilding Copernicus - Disaster Risk Intelligence for Insurers

1) Problem
Extreme water events (floods, droughts, torrential rain) are increasing in frequency and severity.
Insurers struggle with:
- late or fragmented risk signals,
- weak local granularity for underwriting,
- slow event response and loss triage,
- pricing uncertainty in climate-exposed zones.

2) Solution
We build a software platform for insurers and reinsurers that calculates dynamic natural-disaster risk scores at zone and asset level.
The platform combines Copernicus Earth Observation data, Galileo/EGNOS-enabled geolocation, and AI-based risk modeling.

Core capabilities:
- Pre-event underwriting score: location-specific risk profile for pricing and portfolio steering.
- During-event monitoring: near-real-time hazard tracking and alerting.
- Post-event triage: impact prioritization for faster operational decisions.
- Parametric trigger designer: configurable objective thresholds for rapid payout products.

3) Copernicus and Galileo/EGNOS Data Usage
Copernicus inputs:
- Sentinel-1 SAR for all-weather flood extent and surface water dynamics.
- Sentinel-2 optical for vegetation stress and moisture proxies.
- Copernicus climate and rainfall baselines for anomaly detection.

Galileo/EGNOS inputs:
- precise geotagging of field observations and IoT sensor points,
- improved integrity and trust in location-based trigger validation,
- better geo-accuracy for event evidence and claims prioritization workflows.

4) Target Customers and Users
Primary customers:
- P&C insurers,
- reinsurers,
- MGA/parametric insurance providers.

Primary users:
- catastrophe underwriting teams,
- portfolio risk analysts,
- claims operations teams,
- parametric product managers.

5) MVP Scope (48 Hours)
MVP objective:
Deliver a working risk intelligence prototype for one pilot region and one event type (flood-first, drought-ready).

MVP features:
- Interactive risk map with zone-level risk score (Low/Medium/High).
- Time-based anomaly panel (rainfall and moisture deviations from baseline).
- Configurable alert threshold (for underwriting watchlist and event response).
- Simple API endpoint returning risk score for a selected coordinate/zone.
- One demo scenario showing decision impact on pricing or prioritization.

Suggested architecture:
- Data ingestion: Copernicus catalog and selected products.
- Processing: geospatial feature extraction and risk scoring.
- Backend API: risk query and alert endpoints.
- Frontend: map dashboard with explainable risk factors.

6) Why This Is Different (vs existing market solutions)
This is not just another catastrophe map.
Our differentiation:
- European-first stack using Copernicus + Galileo/EGNOS by design.
- Combined flood + drought intelligence in one product logic.
- Explainable risk score with factor-level contribution visibility.
- Parametric trigger prototyping built into the same workflow.
- Fast integration path via API for insurer pricing and operations teams.

7) Business Value
